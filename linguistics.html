Linguistics

Study language structure, meaning, and communication across cultures

About Our Linguistics Programs

Our linguistics programs explore the scientific study of language, covering everything from phonetics and syntax to sociolinguistics and computational linguistics. These programs are perfect for students interested in language, communication, and cultural studies.

Introduction to Linguistics

6 weeks Beginner Hybrid

Explore the fundamentals of linguistics including phonetics, syntax, semantics, and language acquisition. This program provides a comprehensive introduction to the study of language.

What You'll Learn:

  • Phonetics and phonology
  • Morphology and word formation
  • Syntax and sentence structure
  • Semantics and meaning
  • Language acquisition
  • Language families and typology
  • Historical linguistics
Register Now

Sociolinguistics Workshop

5 weeks Intermediate In-person

Study the relationship between language and society, including language variation, dialects, and language policy. This workshop explores how language reflects and shapes social identity.

What You'll Learn:

  • Language variation and dialects
  • Social factors in language use
  • Language and identity
  • Multilingualism and code-switching
  • Language policy and planning
  • Language and gender
  • Language contact and change
Register Now

Computational Linguistics

8 weeks Advanced Hybrid

Learn how linguistics intersects with computer science, including natural language processing and machine translation. This program combines linguistic theory with computational methods.

What You'll Learn:

  • Natural language processing basics
  • Text analysis and parsing
  • Machine translation
  • Speech recognition and synthesis
  • Corpus linguistics
  • Language modeling
  • Applications in AI and chatbots
